Training Philosophy

As I see the years passing by, my training volume seems to be diminishing. I know everyone is busy with family, friends and kids school commitments academically but also for co-curricular and the extras we have willingly added so I don’t expect any sympathy whatsoever. But I must say there are some days when I only have 20minutes and I need to make the most of it. Yes, there are also days when I have all the time in the world but I also want to fit in lunch and maybe a snooze after lunch therefore I don’t have time to warm up thoroughly and even more importantly, cool down and stretch post training sessions.

Note to self: Do something physical every day, Walk-Run, Swim, Gym indoor or outdoor and remember, has my physical output demands dictated my nutritional input demand. Not forgetting to check in mentally once a day asking myself or asking a friend or colleague how you think I am going or how I might have handled a certain stressful situation. As for my sport fanatical teenage boys and a strong willed 8 year old daughter who keeps telling me she can keep up and can go above and beyond her brothers when it comes to most tasks. I still have not been able to find a way to discourage her from thinking otherwise however that sits fine with me!!

For these Ninja Warriors its about:

PARTICIPATION

Getting involved in as many varieties of activity as possible, from Sport – music-drama and maybe debating. As much as mum and dad can tolerate at least.

CONSISTENT

Turn up and train every time scheduled training is on. If you can do extras in your own time then commit to doing this.

MAKE MISTAKES

Understand its not about getting it perfect, it’s about working it out and learning where all the pieces to the puzzle are.

HONESTY

Be true to the sporting moral pillars, Fairness, Equality, Sportsmanship and integrity are the main ones which resinate with me. If I lean on these pillars I know that I will consistently participate at the best of my ability and know that I am not perfect and will make mistakes which will allow me to become technically and tactically better as a physically competent healthy Ninja Warrior.
